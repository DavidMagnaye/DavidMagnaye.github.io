DEFINITIONS

GLOSSARY OF TERMS

Here are some basic terms and definitions that you will encounter in trigonometry.

TERM DEFINITION
arccosine -it outputs the possible angles given an input of a real number value of a cosine
arcsine -it outputs the possible angles given an input of a real number value of a sine
arctangent -it outputs the possible angles given an input of a real number value of a tangent
cosecant -the reciprocal of the value of the sine of an angle
cosine -the ratio of the length of the side adjacent to the given angle to the length of the hypotenuse of the right triangle
cotangent -the reciprocal of the value of the tangent of an angle
hyperbolic functions -"a function of an angle expressed as a relationship between the distances from a point on a hyperbola to the origin and to the coordinate axes, as hyperbolic sine or hyperbolic cosine: often expressed as combinations of exponential functions. (Dictionary.com, n.d.)"
inverse function -a function obtained from setting the dependent variable of one function as the independent variable of another; for instance, if f(x) = y, then g(y) = x.
inverse trigonometric function -a function that is an inverse of a trigonometric function; gives outputs in degrees or radians
radians -standard unit of measuring angles; pi radians = 180 degrees
secant -the reciprocal of the value of the cosine of an angle
sine -the ratio of the length of the side opposite to the given angle to the length of the hypotenuse of the right triangle
SOH-CAH-TOA -a mnemonic for Sine: Opposite Hypotenuse - Cosine: Adjacent Hypotenuse - Tangent: Opposite Adjacent
tangent -the ratio of the lengths of the sides opposite to and adjacent to the given angle, respectively
trigonometric function -a function of an angle that gives a ratio of the lengths of two sides of a right triangle having the said angle; gives outputs in real numbers
trigonometry -the branch of mathematics dealing with the relations of the sides and angles of triangles and with the relevant functions of any angles
