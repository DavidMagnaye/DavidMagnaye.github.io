TRIGONOMETRY

trig · o · nom · e · try

NOUN

-The branch of mathematics dealing with the relations of the sides and angles of triangles and with the relevant functions of any angles. (Lexico, n.d.)

History of Trigonometry

The word trigonometry was coined from two Greek words: trigonon meaning "triangle", and metria meaning "measure".

  • The idea of trigonometry originated from Egyptians that were building pyramids.
  • Trigonometry was developed to aid in the navigation of sailors and astronomers.
  • The very first recorded use of trigonometry was known to be from Hipparchus, a Hellenistic mathematician that recorded the first table of chords back in 150 BCE.
  • Another mathematician, Ptolemy, also made and developed trigonometric advancements way back in 100 CE.
  • An Indian mathematician, Aryabhata, developed the table of half-chords in 499 CE, which are known today as sine tables and cosine tables. This includes the sine, cosine, versine, and inverse sine.
  • In the 10th century, Abul Wafa, a Persian mathematician, introduced the tangent function. He also improved the ways of computing for the trigonometric tables.
  • Then, in 1595, Bartholemaeus Pitiscus, published his works in trigonometry which were considered influential. The works may have had the first recorded use of the coined term "trigonometry".

Fast Facts about Trigonometry

  • Trigonometry has many real-life applications. Trigonometry may be used in oceanography, music, architecture, engineering, computer science, and many other fields.
  • Trigonometry is widely regarded as a branch of geometry, another branch of mathematics that deals with shapes, sizes, angles, positions, and dimensions of objects.
  • There are 6 trigonometric functions, namely, sine, cosine, tangent, secant, cosecant, & cotangent.
  • Correspondingly, the six trigonometric functions have their respective inverses, namely, arcsine, arccosine, arctangent, arcsecant, arccosecant, & arccotangent.
  • Given two side lengths of a triangle and the angle between them, you could find the length of the third side.
  • Several trigonometric laws and identities were discovered and proven by mathematicians to aid in the field of geometry.
